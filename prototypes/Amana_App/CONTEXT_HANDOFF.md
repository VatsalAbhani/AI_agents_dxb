**Can a free zone company invoice mainland UAE clients for services?**
- Since **Dubai Executive Council Resolution No. 11 of 2025 (March 2025)**, free zone cos can operate onshore via DET permits without a 2nd entity: **branch (AED 10k/yr), remote branch permit (AED 10k/yr), or temporary permit (AED 5k/6 mo)**. Regularization deadline was March 3, 2026.
- Remote/digital delivery (SaaS) to mainland is widely done directly from free zones. Physically performing services at mainland client sites triggers the permit. **Mainland DED = no restrictions at all.**

**Shortlist read for an AI company:** **in5** (if accepted — cheapest credible Dubai tech address) · **DIFC AI Licence** (best AI branding/ecosystem, but data-law obligations) · **IFZA/Meydan** (cheapest frictionless Dubai LLC) · **mainland DED** (~AED 50k, zero market-access friction). DMCC only if banking/enterprise prestige matters.

### 4.2 Corporate tax & VAT (verified vs FTA/PwC)

- **Corporate tax:** 9% on taxable income above AED 375,000; 0% below. **Everyone must register** (late-registration penalty AED 10,000).
- **Small Business Relief (SBR):** elect zero taxable income if revenue ≤ AED 3M — **but only for tax periods ending on or before 31 Dec 2026, no extension announced as of July 2026.** ⚠️ **TRAP:** a company incorporated July 2026 typically has its first tax period end in 2027 → may never use SBR unless first period is structured to close 31 Dec 2026. **Get tax advice at incorporation to deliberately structure a short first period.**
- **Qualifying Free Zone Person (0%):** 0% only on "qualifying income"; **B2B services to mainland UAE are generally NON-qualifying (taxed at 9%)**. De minimis = lower of 5% of revenue or AED 5M before losing QFZP entirely. QFZPs need audited financials + substance and **can't elect SBR**. → For a services company selling to mainland UAE, free-zone 0% is mostly irrelevant; effectively same 9%-above-375k as mainland.
- **VAT:** mandatory at **AED 375,000** taxable supplies; voluntary at **AED 187,500**. Standard rate 5%. Late-registration fine AED 10,000.
- **E-invoicing (new):** voluntary from July 2026; mandatory B2B for ≥AED 50M revenue from 1 Jan 2027; **all remaining businesses from 1 July 2027** (appoint Accredited Service Provider by 31 Mar 2027). Budget small SaaS cost in 2027.

### 4.3 Selling B2B in the UAE without a network

- **Relationship-first market** — deals close on trust and face time; cold digital funnels underperform vs warm intros, but decision-makers are unusually accessible.
- **WhatsApp is a legitimate B2B channel** (vendor claim: ~70% of business comms, 90%+ daily open). Norm: LinkedIn connect → move to WhatsApp → meet in person → close. WhatsApp Business number is table stakes.
- **LinkedIn:** ~10M UAE members; primary digital prospecting channel. Personalized outreach works; generic automation is saturated.
- **Events (verified):** Step Conference Dubai (11–12 Feb 2026, DIC; expect Feb 2027); **GITEX Global 7–11 Dec 2026** with **Expand North Star co-located 8–10 Dec 2026** (Dubai Exhibition Centre). Visitor pass ~AED 1,600; 2026 startup pod pricing not yet published.
- **Communities:** in5 / DIC / TECOM ecosystem, DIFC AI Campus, Dubai Chambers events, nationality business councils (Indian Business & Professional Council, British Business Group) = warm-intro engines for newcomers.
- **AI implementation deal sizes (UAE 2025–26):** basic chatbot from ~AED 12,000; typical single-service builds AED 30,000–120,000; mid-large consulting AED 147k–1.4M; enterprise programs AED 500k+. Maintenance adds 25–40%/yr. Hourly: $100–150 junior, $300–500+ senior.
- **Payment culture:** 30–90+ day terms normal; ~55% of B2B invoices paid late (vendor surveys). Defenses: **40–50% advance, milestone billing, keep 3–6 months runway.**

### 4.4 Hiring

- **Junior dev (Dubai):** AED 8,000–16,000/mo (published ranges AED 144k–192k/yr). Add visa (AED 3,500–5,000), mandatory insurance, gratuity (21 days'/yr) → total ≈ salary + 10–20%.
- **Offshore contractors (what most micro-startups do):** India $10–25/hr, Pakistan $15–30/hr, Philippines $20–40/hr; dedicated FT offshore dev $3,000–9,000/mo via agency or $1,500–3,000/mo direct for juniors. A free zone company can pay foreign contractors with no UAE visa/labor obligations.
- **1-visa free zone license supports early stage — yes**, that's the standard setup. Flexi-desk packages carry 1–3 visa quota; offshore contractors consume no quota.

### 4.5 Data/AI regulation & government procurement

- **UAE PDPL (Federal Decree-Law 45/2021):** on the books since Jan 2022 but **Executive Regulations still NOT issued as of 15 June 2026**; enforcement dormant. **Major June 2026 change:** on **14 June 2026** UAE announced the **Federal Authority for Artificial Intelligence and Data** (consolidates AI Office, TDRA digital-gov, Emirates Data Office) — expected to finalize PDPL regs and start enforcement (~6 months after regs publish). Build PDPL-aligned practices now.
- **DIFC (only if you incorporate there):** DP Law No. 5/2020 + **Regulation 10 (2023)** on autonomous systems — transparency notices, AI use-case register, Autonomous Systems Officer, certification scheme. Trade-off if picking DIFC AI Licence.
- **No horizontal "UAE AI Act" exists.** UAE AI Charter (June 2024) = 12 non-binding principles shaping procurement/vendor due diligence. **Dubai AI Seal** certification helps credibility selling AI to Dubai entities. ⚠️ Online claims of a "UAE AI Act enacted March 2026" are **fabricated/AI-generated — do NOT rely on them** (contradicted by White & Case, Morgan Lewis).
- **Government procurement reality for an expat solo founder:** Dubai's 10% SME procurement set-aside and the **Emirati Supplier Programme / Tejar Dubai are for Emirati-owned businesses — do NOT apply to expats.** Expat paths: register on gov supplier portals, subcontract to established primes, pursue Dubai AI Seal, use in5/DIFC AI Campus as channel into gov-adjacent deals.
